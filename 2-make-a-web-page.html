Intro

Comedy is something near and dear to my heart. I tried to make it big. But then I didn't. No worries, though.

The following is the structure I've given to comedy over the years.

Jimmy's Informal Definition and High Level Analysis

Informal defintion - Comedy is merely an unmet expectation that evokes or results in a positive response. There was a book I read that said that comedy started in our early hunting days. If we thought a threat was coming, but it turned out to be something tiny or non-threatening, we'd laugh. There's probably a better definition somewhere.

  1. The setup - The comic gives you the information.
  2. The calculation - The comic gives you time to calculate your expectation.
  3. The punchline - The comic defies your expectation resulting in a tickled funny bone. Guffaws abound.

Comedy Styles

Standup

A person standing on stage telling stories through the lens of their personal experiences. It's essentially public speaking with a higher joke ratio quotient.

Improv

A group of people on stage telling quick stories through the lens of the characters they've just created with nothing more than their voices and mime.

Sketch

A group of people on stage telling quick stories through the lens of the characters they've created and tested. They also usually get props and a chair.


Sitcom

A group of people on scren telling medium-sized stories through the lens of the characters they've created and tested. They also get sets and stuff.

Movie

A group of people on screen telling long stories through the lens of the characters they've created and tested. They also get catering and stuff.

Parody

A person with songwriting ability changing the words of a song either to make fun of that song or some topical event.

Basic Joke Theory

  1. Stay ambiguous
  2. Go from good to bad, bad to worse
  3. Juxtapose things that are oxymoronical
  4. A lot of comedy is built around justification, which is usually based on doublespeak or connecting two unrelated objects in a fun way.
